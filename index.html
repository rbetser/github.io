Bio

A concise overview of academic and industrial research directions.

I am a researcher working at the intersection of machine learning theory, representation learning, and trustworthy AI. In my PhD work, I study the geometric and probabilistic structure of learned representations, including Gaussian structure induced by contrastive learning, likelihood-based modeling in pretrained vision encoders, and training-free detection of generated images and videos. In parallel, as a Senior Researcher at Fujitsu Research of Europe, I work on AI security, safety, and trust, with projects on LLM policy compliance, safe tool use, and risk mitigation for agentic systems.

Current Focus

  • Geometric structure of representations in contrastive and self-supervised learning.
  • Likelihood-based zero-shot detection of generated images and videos.
  • Shared latent structure across encoders and generators.
  • Policy compliance, tool-risk mitigation, and trustworthy deployment of LLM agents.

Research Vision

A recurring theme in my work is that modern AI systems often exhibit latent structure that can be made explicit and useful. On the academic side, this appears through Gaussianity, geometry, and likelihood in representation spaces. On the trustworthy AI side, it appears through principled interfaces between models, tools, and policies that improve safety without sacrificing utility.

PhD Research

Representation structure and geometry, with emphasis on Gaussian structure, likelihoods, and zero-shot detection.

W-CLIP

Likelihoods in CLIP Space

A whitening-based probabilistic view of pretrained CLIP embeddings, enabling a simple likelihood surrogate for images and captions without training. The work connects representation geometry to practical likelihood estimation in foundation models.

Project link Paper link

CLIDE

Generated Image Detection

A general and domain-specific zero-shot detector for generated images based on conditional likelihood. CLIDE adapts the local likelihood model to the domain of the input through conditioning on similar real images.

Project link Paper link

InfoNCE Induces Gaussian Structure

Theory of Contrastive Learning

A theoretical study of why Gaussian structure emerges in contrastive representation learning. The project develops population-level arguments linking alignment, uniformity, and asymptotic Gaussian behavior.

Project link Paper link

UNE

Universal Normal Embedding

A unifying hypothesis that modern encoders and generators interact with a shared approximately Gaussian latent space. The framework aims to explain shared semantic structure across different model families.

Project link Paper link

STALL

Generated Video Detection

A training-free method for detecting generated videos via spatial and temporal likelihoods. By jointly modeling appearance realism and motion consistency, STALL provides robust zero-shot video detection.

Project link Paper link

Broader Theme

Representation Geometry

Across these projects, the broader goal is to better understand the geometry of learned representation spaces and to turn that understanding into practical tools for analysis, detection, and interpretation.

Industry Research

AI security, safety, and trust at Fujitsu Research of Europe.

Trustworthy LLM Systems

My work in industry focuses on securing and governing modern AI systems, especially LLM-based agents. This includes policy compliance, safe orchestration, and mechanisms that improve reliability in real deployment settings.

Selected Topics

  • LLM policy compliance and behavioral control.
  • CAIR and related work on trustworthy AI system behavior.
  • AgenTRIM: mitigation of tool-related risks in agentic AI.
  • Safety-aware interfaces for tool use, permissions, and risk judgment.
